About Me



My name is Lesedi Maleka. I was born in a small town known as Schweizer-Reneke, North West. I later moved to Johannesburg where most of my paternal family lives. I am a Junior Developer with a few months experience in creating and developing websites. A little bit about myself;I consider myself to be an intelligent person, and love fun-facts that can be irrelevant to most.

I love what I do, and I am passionate about coding, and causes me to challenge myself on a daily basis. It is the driving force behind what I do. It is my motivation to strive to improve with every project. Coding has for years been a male dominated field, however there are more women in programming who are making significant contributions to the field. This knowledge has taught me the importance of having the perseverance to change things. Therefore, I will strive towards making positive changes.

By adapting and being patient in solving complex problems,I have developed multiple skills and learnt to find solutions.It is my hope that my successes and achievements will further motivate more women to consider this field. It would be a great priviledge and pleasure if I could make direct and meaningful contributions to the empowerment of women.

Don’t be fooled, I’m not just a programmer, I am so much more. Outside of programming and in terms of hobbies, I enjoy watching soccer, taking photographs and reading fictional books.


I am an ADA graduate!



I have studied front-end development at the African Developers Academy. It was an invaluable experience and I really enjoyed it. I made mistakes and learnt from them - they have helped me to grow as a person. Working in groups has taught me the importance of teamwork, and how much my contributions can add value. I am willing to go the extra mile in order to achieve the objectives and goals. I learnt to put more effort into my job and my projects.

As a graduate from ADA, programming languages have affected the way I think about things. I am confident that the skills I have acquired during the learnership program will help me in my endeavour to become an exceptional coder. The journey has just begun but I am looking forward to new and exciting challenges ahead.
