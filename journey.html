My coding journey

I made contact with Harambee for help in seeking employment.They are an organisation committed to help young people to realise their dreams. After some skills assessments, I received a text message to come to their offices in Johannesburg, CBD. After listening to a motivational talk on the day, I proceeded to do some tests in the computer room. After that test, I was asked if I would like to take part in the maths and physics tests.

I was called in a few times to write more tests. The wait during the unemployment phase felt like a lifetime. Later I was invited to attend a game day at the African Developers Academy hosted by Jozi Hub. They used the games to evaluate if I had certain skills such as being a team player, social and creative skills, and general engagement. After this, I again had to do more testing and I was invited to a bootcamp; I was one of 30 out of 100 people that were selected.

Next, we moved on to Turtle Academy and did some exercises to determine if we possessed problem solving skills, and the willingness to learn. I was one of the final 12 that was chosen for the learnership program at the African Developers Academy. It is here where I had the opportunity to learn about programming for the first time.

First we learned HTML. I had never coded before and my mind was blown! At times I felt overwhelmed and experienced information overload. I developed processes that worked for me during this learning phase, and I began to understand things better. For instance, I took manual notes and asked many clarity seeking questions. After about two weeks I got the hang of it. The more I practiced, the easier it became, and I started enjoying the experience.

Shortly after this, we learned to style websites and make them responsive with CSS. This was when I saw the potential of web designing and really fell in love with making beautiful user experiences. …My best part was when I learnt the aspect of animation within CSS. I was fascinated by the things I was able to do with animation.

Finally we honed in on JavaScript. This was the most I’d been pushed since trigonometry in high school, but I learned to love the challenge and I grew from the experience. …It was by far the most challenging programming language, but the most rewarding experience. My knowledge of user interface and functionality improved in leaps and bounds. I learnt other skills which enabled me to simplify processes and still achieve desirable results.

My last few months at ADA was when the CoronaVirus was announced as a worldwide pandemic. This meant that we couldn’t come into class for a whole semester and we missed out on the valuable experience of a 2 month internship. I spent every day in lockdown learning on my phone through platforms such as Free-Code Camp.

Where to from here? ...My next move is to get involved in more programming projects. Hopefully get an opportunity to learn Back-End Development, and become a brilliant Full-Stack Developer. I would like to share my skills by teaching the young ones the art of coding.
